    🏝️ Culebra Quick-Start Guide

    ⛴️ Getting Here

    Ferry from Ceiba Terminal is the most popular route. Passenger-only takes ~45 min, cargo ferry ~1.5 hrs.

    Book tickets at puertoricoferry.com — they sell out fast on weekends!
    You MUST have a return ride arranged BEFORE traveling. Uber doesn't work FROM Ceiba.

    By Air: Air Flamenco from Condado (Isla Grande) — only 27 minutes!

    🛴 Getting Around

    No Uber, no Lyft, limited taxis. A rental is essentially mandatory.

    VehicleBest ForReaches

    Golf carts are PROHIBITED at Punta Soldado, Playa Datiles, and Playa Zoni. You will get stuck.
    An e-bike costs less than a single taxi ride and takes you everywhere. Book instantly on Peek or visit todoculebra.com

    🍕 Hungry? We Deliver!

    No vehicle? No problem. ShopCulebra delivers groceries, pizza from Jibara, and Chinese food island-wide.

    💵 Money & Cell Service

    • Cash is king — many places are cash only. ONE ATM on the island (Banco Popular, pinned on map).
    • AT&T and T-Mobile work in Dewey. Signal drops to zero on east coast and remote beaches.
    • Your map works offline — save the page to your phone before heading out!

    🏖️ Beach Rules

    • No glass bottles on any beach — cans or plastic only
    • Pack out everything — no trash cans at remote beaches
    • Reef-safe sunscreen ONLY — regular sunscreen destroys coral
    • Don't touch coral, turtles, or marine life
    • No lifeguards — swim with a buddy
    Best snorkeling: Playa Melones, Carlos Rosario, Playa Tamarindo — all pinned on the map!

    🎒 What to Pack

    • Reef-safe sunscreen
    • Cash (at least $200)
    • Reusable water bottle
    • Snorkel gear (rentals available too)
    • Beach shoes for rocky entries
    • Dry bag for phone on water taxis
    • Portable charger — power outages happen
    • Insect repellent for dusk
